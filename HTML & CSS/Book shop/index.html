    My Suggested Reads


    I haven't always been the most adventurous book reader but I can say, one has found comfort in a certified good read throughout this quarantine. We stan escapism! But anyways here's the list:




    ="Queenie"Queenie


    by Candice Carty-Williams

    £6.99

    Open, warm and immensely funny, Carty-Williams’s sparkling debut is an unforgettable coming-of-age tale that explores themes such as; black female identity and sexual politics in contemporary Britain.



    Ghana must Go


    by Taiye Selasi

    £3.99

    “Ghana Must Go” tells the story of Folasadé Savage, who leaves Lagos for Pennsylvania, where she meets her Ghanaian husband, Kweku Sai, a brilliant surgeon. Fola, as she is called, gives up her dream of going to law school in order to raise their four children.



    Behold the Dreamers


    by Imbolo Mbue


    £5.99


    A NEW YORK TIMES BOOK OF THE YEAR AN OBSERVER BOOK OF THE YEAR OPRAH BOOK CLUB PICK A powerful and timely story of marriage, class, race and the pursuit of the American Dream. Behold the Dreamers is a dazzling debut novel about life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness – and of what we’re prepared ...

    What we Lose

    by Zinzi Clemmons

    £4.99

    A short, intense and profoundly moving novel about race, identity, family, sex, country, and death – from one of the National Book Foundation’s 5 Under 35: an author of rare, haunting power this is a stunning debut about a young African-American woman coming of age. Raised in Pennsylvania, Thandi views the world of her mother’s childhood in Johannesburg as both impossibly distant and ever present. She is an outsider wherever she goes, caught between being black and white...
